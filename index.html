EV Charger Coverage Map — Alberta

About This App

Charger Station Data

Charging station data is sourced from two providers:

  • Primary: NREL Alternative Fuels Station Locator — maintained by the National Renewable Energy Laboratory as part of the Alternative Fuels Data Center (AFDC).
  • Supplemental: Open Charge Map — a community-driven, non-profit charger database that fills in missing stations and power ratings.

The merged dataset includes 938 public EV charging stations in Alberta with power ratings (kW) for 90% of stations. Data was last refreshed on March 14, 2026.

Route Planner

Road-following routes are provided by the OSRM demo server (Open Source Routing Machine). Charging time estimates are based on battery size, state of charge, and the station's maximum power rating. Estimates assume constant power delivery and do not account for charging curve taper above 80% SOC.

Map Tiles

Map tiles provided by OpenStreetMap contributors.

Range Calculations

  • Temperature degradation: Linear model from 100% at 20°C to 50% at −20°C and below. Based on real-world cold weather data from AAA and Recurrent Auto studies.
  • Highway speed loss: Based on aerodynamic drag increasing with speed squared. At 120 km/h, approximately 28% range loss compared to the 90 km/h baseline.
  • Battery reserve: Subtracts a safety buffer so you don't arrive at 0%.
  • Starting charge: Accounts for the common recommendation not to charge above 80%.
  • Round-trip safe: Halves the effective range, ensuring you can reach a charger and return.

Risk Coloring

  • Green (3+ chargers): Low risk — multiple DC fast chargers available.
  • Yellow (2 chargers): Moderate risk — if one is in use or broken, you may wait.
  • Red (1 charger): High risk — single point of failure.
  • Blue (Level 2): Slow charging only (~7 kW), not practical for road trips.

Charger count is based on DC fast charging ports only, excluding CHAdeMO connectors (deprecated standard).

Coverage Calculation

Coverage percentage is calculated by sampling ~17,000 grid points across Alberta's land area and checking how many fall within the adjusted range of at least one active charging station, using the Haversine distance formula.

Open Source

Source code available on GitHub. Built with Leaflet.js and vanilla JavaScript. No backend, no frameworks.
